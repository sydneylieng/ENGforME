Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering is the practical application of chemistry and is the type of engineering degree which involves technology which would utilize chemical reactions to address various problems. A chemical engineer would also create products such as: cosmetics, foods, pharmaceuticals, beverages, cleaners, etc. This engineering field is a very broad degree which allows for a number of different forms of careers.

Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering is the development of different infrastructures such as buildings, railways, roads, bridges and also involves general construction project management. Aside from general construction, Civil Engineers play an important role in rebuilding projects as well. In the case of a natural disaster, Civil Engineers would need to help rebuild while also improving design to prevent it from happening again in the future. Civil Engineers may work in the private or public sector at any level as it is a very broad engineering degree. Environmental Engineering, Structural Engineering and Marine Engineering are all specialties of Civil Engineering.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical Engineering involves the study of various forms of energy, such as electrical, hydro, and naturally occurring energy sources which provide wind and solar energy. An Electrical Engineer would develop technology which will assist with the application of these different forms of energy. Electrical Engineers design components for electronic equipment, communication systems, power grids, automobiles, etc.

Computer and Software Engineering

Computer engineering focus on the Information Technology (IT) industry. It is a combination between both computer sciences and electrical engineering. Their main focus is to embed computers in other machines and systems, build networks to transfer data, and develop ways to make computers, faster, smaller, and more capable. Potential jobs for Computer Engineering graduates are Telecommunications, Networking, Software Application Development, or Manufacturing.

Software Engineers specialize in designing, building, testing, and maintaining the software in computer systems. Various computer systems that Software Engineers deal with include systems that control electrical and mechanical devices, communication systems, systems which are used to design engineering products, financial systems, and information systems. There is a high demand for this field of engineering as software is essentially used in every type of organization and in the designing of various devices.

Biomedical Engineering

Biomedical engineering is the study of two fields, medicine and biology. Biomedical Engineers would apply their design skills to these two fields. This is often done to assist in advancing healthcare treatment technology. These engineers would develop and maintain diagnostic devices which includes EEGs, MRIs, etc. Physicians use would these machines to diagnosis various medical problems/issues.

Engineering Physics

Physics combines engineering with physics and allows students to study subjects where these two areas intersect. This program is a precursor to graduate studies in physics or engineering. Graduate students may work in research on other fields of engineering like mechanical or nuclear. The demand for this type of graduate is relatively high.
